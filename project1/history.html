History of the Outer Banks

Discover the rich history and heritage of the Outer Banks.

Early Settlement

The Outer Banks have a long history, beginning with Native American tribes like the Algonquians, who lived on the islands before European explorers arrived. In the 1580s, the English attempted to settle the area with the Lost Colony on Roanoke Island, led by Sir Walter Raleigh. This early settlement mysteriously disappeared, leaving behind the word "CROATOAN" carved into a tree, sparking one of historyâ€™s most famous unsolved mysteries.

Shipwrecks and Lighthouses

Due to the treacherous waters off the coast, many shipwrecks occurred, leading to the construction of iconic lighthouses, which still stand today as symbols of the area's maritime history.

Cultural Heritage

The cultural heritage of the Outer Banks is rich with traditions from fishing, farming, and the sea. Explore the stories of the communities that have shaped this unique landscape.
